Side Projects

  • The Great Divider TheGreatDivider

    I launched a web application that sifts through reddit conversations to identify common discussion topics within specific political communities. First, I implemented a version of TextRank using tf-idf features and cosine similarity to find keywords from reddit comments. Armed with the ability to extract keywords, I built a pipeline that performs hierarchical clustering to locate small clusters of keywords representing discussion topics, and I hand-coded scoring based on silhouette coefficients to evaluate the results. The whole project was completed in Python, with help from the reddit API, PostreSQL, scikit-learn, and Flask.

  • Who said it? obama-or-bush

    In this project, I explored speeches given by Obama and Bush through webscraping, natural language processing, and machine learning in Python. Using a dataset of scraped transcripts, I trained a support vector classifier to identify whether a radio address was given by Obama or Bush with greater than 99% accuracy. I developed a front end that allows users to compete against this model and explore its decisions through interactive plots. Along the way, I experimented with Bayesian optimization for hyperparameter tuning. The model was built with scikit-learn and MOE using tools from NLTK, the data was obtained with Beautiful Soup, and the frontend was created with Flask and D3.js.

  • Coffee & Demographics coffee-and-demographics

    I developed a Shiny application that describes one approach to working with geospatial data and the U.S. Census. Guided by interactive maps, this carefully details the creation of a dataset that addresses how the spread and density of coffee shops in major U.S. cities is related to their demographic characteristics. All processing was completed in R, using sp and three related packages for managing spatial objects.
